About Aether Instruments

Handcrafted tongue drums and flutes, built one at a time in a small Arizona shop — where music, craft, and soul all meet.

For more than fourteen years, I’ve been immersed in the craft of building musical instruments by hand. My journey began in woodworking, where I spent many years creating guitars, banjos, ukuleles, bass guitars, and cigar box guitars. When I discovered tongue drums, I also discovered a love for metalworking, and since then I’ve built over fifty tongue drums and a couple dozen flutes. Every instrument is shaped slowly, intentionally, and with deep respect for the materials and the music they will one day carry.

I’ve always believed that music has the power to heal and restore the soul. That belief guides everything I make. My goal is to create instruments that are both functional and artistic—objects of beauty meant to be held, admired, and played. I design them to be accessible to beginners while still offering depth and richness for seasoned musicians. At the end of the day, I simply love working with my hands and sharing instruments that invite people into the joy of music.

For me, building instruments is more than a craft—it’s a quiet spiritual journey. The scent of wood shavings, the ring of shaped metal, the subtle shift of grain beneath my fingertips… all of it pulls me into a place of calm reflection. Even when I’m away from the workbench, my mind drifts back to new shapes, new tones, new creations waiting to be born. Each instrument feels like a conversation between heart, hand, and material—and I’m simply grateful to be a part of it.
